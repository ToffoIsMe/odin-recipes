Rotkohl

Traditional German Rotkohl

This traditional recipe features heaps of red cabbage, shredded and with more flavor than you would ever expect from this simple vegetable

Ingredients

  • 2 Tablespoons butter
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 large Granny Smith apple, peeled and chopped
  • 1 head red cabbage, sliced very thin
  • 3 Tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 cup apple cidde
  • 1 Tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 2 juniper berries
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Melt butter in large dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and appled, saute for 7-10 minutes until soft.
  2. Add the cabbage to the onions and apples. Cook 5 minutes stirring frequently.
  3. Add the red wine vinegar, apple cider, sugar, bay leaf, cloves, juniper, berries, and salt. Bring the liquids to a boil, then cover and reduce head to a simmer.
  4. Simmer for 1 hour until cabbage is soft but not mushy. Serve warm or cold.
